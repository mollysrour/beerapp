The Beer Recommendation App


Choose a beer type from the following options. Read the descriptions below to learn more about different types of beer.

Stout: Stouts are a widespread and varied family of beers whose members all share a deep, dark, roasty character.
Porter: Porters are a wide family of dark brown beers with a creamy, roasty malt.
IPA: IPAs are pale, strong, and bitter, dominated by hops with plenty of malt.
Wheat Ale: Wheat Ales are brewed with a large proportion of wheat relative to the amount of malted barley.
Pale Ale: Pale Ales are brewed with predominantly pale malt, characteristically hoppy.
Sour: Sours have an intentionally acidic, tart or sour taste.
Strong Ale: Strong ales are deep amber in color with a sweet malty palate and can be somewhat fruity.
Pilsner: Pilsners are pale lager beers with strong hop flavor.
Dark Lager: Dark lagers are crisp beers that get their dark color from unroasted dark malts.
Brown Ale: Brown Ales are toasty and nutty in flavor, with less hops than a pale ale.
